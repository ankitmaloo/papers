Convergence to Fixed Points

In standard feedforward models, the representation of a token continually changes as it passes through each distinct layer. However, what happens when you pass the exact same representation through the same block of layers recursively?

Recent mechanistic analysis by Blayney et al. reveals a fascinating phenomenon: Cyclic Recurrence. As representations loop through the recurrent block, each layer within the block eventually converges to a distinct fixed point in the latent space.

The Trajectory of Thought

During the first few loops, the representation experiences significant representation drift. But over time, it spirals towards a stable attractor. Once this fixed point is reached, the behavior of the attention heads stabilizes, and further loops produce constant, predictable behavior.

Stages of Inference

Does looping change how the model processes information? Surprisingly, recurrent blocks learn stages of inference that closely mirror those of standard feedforward models.

  • Stage 1: Initial context building and shallow token mixing.
  • Stage 2: Complex routing and relation extraction.
  • Stage 3: Final aggregation and prediction formation.

The difference is that a looped model repeats these stages in depth with each iteration. Instead of a linear progression, it acts as a deep iterative refinement process, progressively polishing the latent representation until the reasoning path is resolved.
